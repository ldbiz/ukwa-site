Welsh devolution referendum 2011

This collection of websites focuses on the referendum on the powers of the National Assembly for Wales, held on March 3rd 2011. The voters of Wales had approved a very limited form of devolution in September 1997 by a wafer thin majority of 6,721 votes. Following the coalition agreement between Labour and Plaid Cymru, signed after the 2007 Assembly election, a further referendum was held in 2011 on granting the Assembly the power to make laws without having to first seek permission from the UK Parliament. The Yes for Wales campaign was backed by Assembly Members from all 4 main parties, trade unions and many parts of civic society while the True Wales campaign, which urged voters to say No described itself as a grass-roots organisation.
On this occasion the result was far more decisive, with 517,132 voting yes, and 297,380 voting no. The only county to record a no majority was Monmouthshire, by 320 votes.
