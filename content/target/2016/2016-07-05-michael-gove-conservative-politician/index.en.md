Michael Gove stood as Conservative candidate for Surrey Heath at the 2005 general election. He was first elected to serve as MP for Surrey Heath in 2005 and re-elected in 2010 and 2015. He stood for re-election again in 2017 and was successful. Following the 2010 General Election, he served as Secretary of State for Education, and was appointed as Chief Whip in 2014. After the General Election in May 2015, he became Lord Chancellor, Secretary of State for Justice, until July 2016. He took a prominent role in the successful campaign to leave the European Union, as Co-Convenor of the Vote Leave campaign committee. Following the 2017 General Election, Michael was appointed as Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs
