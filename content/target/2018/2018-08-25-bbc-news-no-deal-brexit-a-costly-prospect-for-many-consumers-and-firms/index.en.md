The government's "no deal" papers reveal the complicated exercise of leaving the EU could carry significant costs for consumers and businesses if Britain and the EU fail to agree on a transition period and a subsequent trading agreement. Those increased costs would be very likely to have a negative impact on the economy and could mean higher prices in the shops as firms pass on the higher costs of doing business.
