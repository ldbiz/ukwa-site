‘From “A Penny in the Pound” to “Free at the Point of Delivery”: Cataloguing the pre- and post-1948 hospital records of Monmouthshire’ is a a Wellcome Trust funded cataloguing project at Gwent Archives. Monmouthshire’s hospital records are valuable resources as for they provide into the history of healthcare provision in the area which inspired Aneurin Bevan to found the National Health Service. Prior to 1948, a number of hospitals were built and operated with funds which were accumulated solely from donations and member subscriptions. The catalogue will be an important resources for understanding the social and welfare history of industrial south east Wales from the mid-nineteenth century. This is the official project blog.
