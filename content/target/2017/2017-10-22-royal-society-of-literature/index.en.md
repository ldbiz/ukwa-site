The Royal Society of Literature (RSL) is a learned society. It was founded in 1820 to "reward literary merit and excite literary talent". The society maintains its current level of about 500 Fellows of the Royal Society of Literature: generally 14 new fellows are elected annually, who are accorded the privilege of using the post-nominal letters FRSL. The RSL runs a membership scheme and offers a varied programme of events to members and the general public. The RSL also runs a schools outreach programme in collaboration with the literacy charity First Story.
